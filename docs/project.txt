StageOneInAction Back Office – Web Platform Requirements

User Roles: Administrators & Coaches

1. Platform Overview

The StageOneInAction Back Office is a web-based platform that:

Gives Coaches a structured way to:

Complete onboarding and compliance.

Manage ambassadors.

Track Business Excellence (CRM, website readiness, marketing outreach).

Set weekly goals and track income-generating activities.

Progress toward events and Resource Center ownership.

Request sponsorships and communicate with HQ.

Gives Administrators tools to:

Manage coaches and ambassadors.

Configure onboarding, courses, events, and Business Excellence.

Review sponsorship and Resource Center applications.

Track program performance and generate reports.

This platform supports StageOneInAction’s four pillars:

Personal Development

Professional Development

Talent Development

Community Involvement

2. Roles & Permissions
2.1 Administrators

Capabilities:

Secure login to admin dashboard.

Create, edit, deactivate Coach accounts.

Create and manage Ambassador records (no ambassador login).

Configure:

Onboarding checklists.

Courses and quizzes (e.g., anti-human trafficking, workbook training).

Events and qualification settings.

Business Excellence defaults (CRM, website content, outreach targets).

Review and decide on:

Sponsorship/funding requests.

Resource Center applications.

View global stats and export reports.

Send and reply to messages with coaches.

2.2 Coaches

Capabilities:

Secure login to coach dashboard.

Complete onboarding checklist (including training).

View and manage assigned ambassadors (pending/approved).

Use Business Excellence tools:

CRM status tracking.

Website content submission progress.

Marketing Outreach targets vs actuals.

Set and review weekly goals.

Access the Ambassador Workbook course and materials.

Log and review income-generating activities.

Track progress toward:

Active Coach status.

Event qualification.

Resource Center eligibility.

Submit sponsorship and project funding requests.

Message Administrators for support.

If approved as Resource Center Owner, manage Resource Center activities.

3. Authentication & Access Control
3.1 Login

No public self sign-up.

Admins create Coach accounts.

Login via email/username + password.

Password reset via “Forgot Password” link and email reset flow.

3.2 Role-Based Access

Enforce permissions server-side:

Admin access to admin views only.

Coach sees only their own profile, ambassadors, metrics, and communications.

4. Coach Experience – Functional Requirements
4.1 Coach Dashboard

On login, coach sees a dashboard with:

Onboarding progress bar.

“Pending Ambassadors” notification.

Business Excellence summary (CRM, website, outreach snapshot).

Weekly goals overview.

Recent income entries summary.

Events & qualification status.

Messages/notifications from Admin.

4.2 Coach Onboarding Checklist

Purpose: Guide a coach from “new” to “Active Coach.”

Checklist items (configurable, but minimally include):

Upload profile photo.

Complete coach profile (bio, contact, location).

Watch Anti–Human Trafficking course.

Pass Anti–Human Trafficking assessment.

Complete Mental Fitness assessment.

Confirm receipt of Coach Workbook.

Complete coach interview (status updated by Admin).

Confirm coach fee ($1,500) paid/approved.

Have at least 2 Approved ambassadors assigned.

Functionality:

Each item shows:

Status: Not Started / In Progress / Submitted / Approved.

Action button/link where relevant.

Overall completion percentage.

Display a clear Coach Status:

Onboarding Incomplete / Active Coach.

4.3 Anti–Human Trafficking Course & Assessment

Course:

Dedicated page within portal.

Embedded or linked training video.

“Mark as Watched” action (minimum) or auto-complete after viewing.

Assessment (Quiz):

Multiple-choice questions configured by Admin.

Passing score threshold (e.g., 80%).

Allow retakes until passed.

Store:

Score.

Completion date.

Pass/fail status.

Requirement:

Must be passed for:

Onboarding completion.

Event eligibility (if configured).

4.4 Ambassador Management (Coach View Only)

Coaches do not log in as ambassadors but see their assigned ambassadors.

Ambassador List:

Columns:

Name

Status: Pending / Approved / Inactive / Completed / On Hold

Region

Assessment status (e.g., Not Submitted / Submitted / Reviewed)

Pending Ambassadors Widget:

Top of dashboard:

“You have X pending ambassadors.”

Button: “View Pending Ambassadors.”

When Admin updates an ambassador to Approved, coach sees status updated.

Tie to Onboarding:

Count of “Approved ambassadors” contributes to coach onboarding requirement (≥ 2).

4.5 Weekly Goals & Reflection

Weekly Goals:

Coach can define goals such as:

Number of ambassador check-ins.

Classes taught.

New contacts made.

Follow-ups.

Fields:

Week (auto or selectable).

Goal title.

Target value.

Optional notes.

Tracking Results:

End of week:

Coach enters “Actual” value.

Marks goal as:

Completed / Partially / Not.

UI:

Weekly summary with % of goals completed.

History page of past weeks.

4.6 Ambassador Workbook Course Access

Course Module:

“Ambassador Workbook Course” section includes:

Training videos for coaches on how to use the workbook.

Download links:

Ambassador Workbook PDF.

Implementation/teacher guide.

Tracking:

Status per coach:

Not Started / In Progress / Completed.

Admin may mark completion as required for certain teaching roles or events.

4.7 Income Tracking (Coach)

Purpose: Help coaches see how SOIA-related activities contribute to income.

Income Entry Form:

Fields:

Date.

Activity Type (dropdown):

Own services.

Teaching personal development classes (with at least 4 ambassadors).

Fundraising.

Other (with text description).

Description / notes.

Amount (numeric, no negatives).

Summary View:

Total income:

This month.

Year-to-date.

Breakdown by Activity Type.

Optional simple chart (per month).

4.8 Business Excellence (Coach)

The Business Excellence section sits prominently in the coach dashboard with three main components:

CRM Activation & Subscription

Website Content Submission

Marketing Outreach (Targets vs Actuals)

4.8.1 CRM Activation & Subscription

For each coach, track:

CRM Activated: Yes / No

CRM Subscription Active: Yes / No

CRM Provider: text or dropdown (optional).

Activation Date: date.

Last Login Date: date (if data available or manually maintained).

UI:

A card such as:

CRM Status

Activation: ✅ / ❌

Subscription: ✅ / ❌

Provider: [text]

Activated on: [date]

Last login: [date or “Unknown”]

Admin can update these fields; deeper integration can be added later.

4.8.2 Website Content Submission Checklist

Track the coach’s website-readiness content. Each item has:

Boolean status (Submitted Yes/No).

Content field (text/file).

Items:

Logo

Logo Submitted: Yes/No

File upload or URL.

Services

Services Description Submitted: Yes/No

Text: list/description of services.

Products (if applicable)

Products Info Submitted: Yes/No

Text: list/description.

Pricing

Pricing Submitted: Yes/No

Text: pricing structure/packages.

Target Audience

Target Audience Defined: Yes/No

Text: who they serve (age, region, type of client).

Business Contact

Contact Info Submitted: Yes/No

Fields: email, phone, city/country, social/website (optional).

About

About Section Submitted: Yes/No

Text: brief about the business.

Vision & Mission

Vision/Mission Submitted: Yes/No

Text: vision and mission statements.

Bio

Bio Submitted: Yes/No

Text: coach’s personal/professional bio.

UI:

Show a progress bar for Website Content:

e.g., “7 of 9 items submitted (78%).”

List each item with:

Status toggle or icon.

“Edit” / “Update” button.

4.8.3 Marketing Outreach – Targets & Actuals

Outreach Categories (recommended default):

New Contacts / Conversations.

Follow-Ups.

Invitations/Presentations.

Social Media Posts (intentional promotion).

Referrals / Introductions Requested.

Collaboration / Partnership Outreach (schools, churches, NGOs, etc.).

Targets:

Coach can set Daily / Weekly / Monthly targets (or Admin can pre-load defaults).

Example default targets (for guidance):

Daily:

3 New Contacts

5 Follow-Ups

Weekly:

15 New Contacts

25 Follow-Ups

3 Invitations/Presentations

5 Referrals Requested

2 Collaboration Outreach attempts

3–5 Social Posts

Monthly:

60 New Contacts

100 Follow-Ups

4+ Presentations

8–10 Collaboration Outreach attempts

12–20 Social Posts

Logging Actuals:

“Quick Add Activity” form with:

Date.

Activity Type.

Quantity (e.g., 1 presentation, 3 new contacts).

Short note (optional).

Summary View:

For a given week/month:

This Week – Outreach Summary

New Contacts: Actual / Target

Follow-Ups: Actual / Target

Invitations/Presentations: Actual / Target

Social Posts: Actual / Target

Referrals Requested: Actual / Target

Collaboration Outreach: Actual / Target

Use visual indicators:

Green if Actual ≥ Target.

Yellow if close.

Red if significantly below.

4.9 Resource Center Eligibility & Management
4.9.1 Eligibility Tracking

For each coach, track number of coaches they’ve recruited.

Display in dashboard:

“Coaches Recruited: X / 5”

When X ≥ 5:

Show a message: “You are eligible to apply as a Resource Center Owner.”

Enable “Apply to Become Resource Center Owner” button.

4.9.2 Resource Center Application

Application form fields:

Proposed location (city, region).

Community description.

Vision & goals for the Resource Center.

Capacity info (spaces, facilities if any).

Status:

Not Submitted / Under Review / Approved / Declined (with reason).

4.9.3 Resource Center Management (for approved owners)

If coach is approved as Resource Center Owner:

Unlock Resource Center tab for that coach.

Features:

List of classes/workshops hosted by the center:

Title, date, audience type (ambassadors/adults), attendance count.

Ability to:

Propose new classes (topic, date, audience).

Record attendance after each class.

“Impact Summary”:

Total classes taught.

Total participants served.

4.10 Sponsorship & Project Funding Requests

Coaches initiate these requests from within their portal.

Types:

Coach Sponsorship (e.g., fee, travel).

Ambassador Sponsorship (program fees, travel, resources).

Project Funding (community or business projects).

Fields:

Request Type.

Beneficiary:

Coach self / Ambassador (selected from their list) / Project.

Amount requested.

Amount they can contribute (optional).

Reason (text).

Urgency: Urgent / Normal / Flexible.

Status:

Submitted / Under Review / Approved (full) / Approved (partial) / Payment Plan / Not Approved.

Admin decision and notes displayed to coach.

4.11 Events & Qualification (Coach View)

Events include at least:

Leadership Conference (mid-year).

Youth Convention (after Thanksgiving).

Coach view:

Event name, dates, location, description.

Qualification requirements list (e.g., onboarding complete, certain # of ambassadors, Business Excellence basics).

Coach’s progress:

Requirement-level view: each requirement marked Yes/No.

Event qualification status:

Not Qualified / Almost Qualified / Qualified.

RSVP controls for qualified coaches:

Yes / No / Maybe.

Option to connect to sponsorship request for travel support.

4.12 Messaging & Support

Message Center:

Coaches can:

Create new message threads to Admin.

Select a category (Onboarding, Ambassadors, Sponsorship, Events, Technical, Other).

View replies and full history.

Admin can:

View all threads from coaches.

Reply via portal (coach sees response in portal; optional email alert).

Set status per thread: Open / In Progress / Resolved.

5. Administrator – Functional Requirements
5.1 Admin Dashboard

Admin sees:

Total coaches; Active vs Inactive.

Onboarding completion summary (e.g., X coaches incomplete).

Total ambassadors; status breakdown.

Pending sponsorship requests count.

Pending Resource Center applications count.

Upcoming events and number qualified.

Quick links to messaging queue.

5.2 User Management

Coaches:

Create new coach:

Name, email, region, initial role.

Recruiter coach (if applicable).

Edit coach profile & status (Active/Inactive/Suspended).

Update onboarding fields (interview done, fee confirmed).

Delete/deactivate account.

Ambassadors:

Create or import ambassador records.

Assign ambassadors to coaches.

Update statuses & key fields.

5.3 Onboarding Configuration

Define coach onboarding tasks:

For each task:

Label.

Description.

Type (manual status, video, quiz, upload, boolean).

Required: Yes/No.

Change rules such as:

“Coach is Active when all required items = Approved.”

“Coach must have ≥ 2 Approved ambassadors to be Active.”

5.4 Course & Quiz Configuration

For each course (e.g., Anti–Human Trafficking, Ambassador Workbook):

Define:

Course name, description.

Video URL or embed code.

Configure assessment:

Questions, options, correct answers.

Passing score.

View:

Per-coach course completion and quiz results.

5.5 Business Excellence Configuration & Reporting

Admin can:

Configure default outreach targets (daily/weekly/monthly).

Adjust outreach categories.

View reports on:

CRM activation/subscription across coaches.

Website content completeness (how many are “fully submitted”).

Outreach activity vs targets (e.g., which coaches consistently hit targets).

5.6 Events & Qualification

Admin can:

Create/edit events:

Name, date(s), location, description.

Configure basic qualification flags:

“Require onboarding complete.”

“Require ≥ X approved ambassadors.”

Other simple conditions.

View:

List of coaches and their qualification status per event.

RSVP responses.

5.7 Sponsorship & Project Funding Requests

Admin interface:

Queue of all requests with filters:

Type, coach, region, status.

View details of each request:

Beneficiary, amount, reason, urgency.

Set decision and notes:

Approved (full/partial), payment plan, Not Approved.

Status and notes surfaced to coach.

5.8 Resource Center Applications

Admin can:

View list of Resource Center applications.

Open each application and see:

Coach details, recruited coaches count.

Location, vision, community details.

Approve or decline:

Update coach record as Resource Center Owner if approved.

5.9 Reporting & Exports

Initial report capabilities:

Coaches:

Total, Active vs Inactive, onboarding completion.

Ambassadors:

Total, by coach, status counts.

Sponsorship:

Requests and decisions summary.

Events:

Qualification and RSVP lists.

Business Excellence:

CRM activation stats.

Website content completeness.

Outreach performance (roll-up).

Export as CSV for further processing.

5.10 Messaging Management

Admin tools:

Inbox of all coach messages.

Filter by category and status.

Reply, update thread status.

Optionally assign messages to specific Admin staff (not required in v1, but nice-to-have).

7. Non-Functional Requirements
7.1 Security

HTTPS for all connections.

Secure password hashing.

Role-based access control enforced server-side.

Careful handling of youth-related data (ambassadors).

7.2 Usability

Simple, clear UI; non-technical language.

Works on mobile (responsive design).

Clear buttons & labels: “Start Onboarding,” “Request Sponsorship,” etc.

7.3 Performance

Reasonable load times under normal usage.

Scales to hundreds/thousands of users without major redesign.

7.4 Logging & Audit

Log:

Onboarding task completions.

Sponsorship decisions.

Role changes (e.g., Resource Center Owner).

Maintain timestamps and responsible Admin IDs.

7.5 Notifications

Email notifications for:

New coach account.

Password reset.

Sponsorship decisions.

Resource Center decisions.

New messages (optional).

SMS can be added later; design should not block it.
